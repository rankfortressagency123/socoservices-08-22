1. You Could Get Seriously Injured


Electrical repairs can be hazardous, even deadly. According to the U.S. Consumer Product Safety Commission, electrocution is one of the leading causes of home injury deaths. Hundreds of people are killed yearly, and electrical shocks and burns seriously injure thousands more. So if you don’t know what you’re doing, it’s simply not worth the risk to try to do your electrical work.

But not all electrical repairs are created equal. Some are simple and easy to do yourself, as long as you take the necessary precautions. Here are a few examples of common electrical repairs that almost anyone can handle:

Replacing a Light Fixture: This repair that many people don’t even realize is an option. If you have a light fixture that’s broken or outdated, simply replacing it with a new one is usually all it takes to fix the problem. Just be sure to turn off the power to the fixture at the breaker box before you begin working on it.

Replacing an Outlet: Another surprisingly easy repair is replacing an outlet. If an outlet is no longer working, the problem is likely with the wiring and not the actual outlet. Replacing an outlet is a relatively simple process, but again, you’ll need to ensure the power is turned off at the breaker box before beginning.

Installing a New Light Switch: This repair is slightly more complicated than the others, but it’s still something many people can do. Follow the instructions carefully and turn off the power at the breaker box before starting.

Of course, some electrical repairs are simply too dangerous to attempt alone. If you’re ever in doubt, it’s always best to call a professional. But for the minor, simple repairs listed above, there’s no reason you can’t do them yourself and save yourself some money in the process.

2. You Could Cause a Fire


Fires are another serious concern when it comes to electrical repairs. Improper wiring is a leading cause of house fires, and faulty electrical work is often to blame. If you’re planning on doing any electrical repairs yourself, be sure to know what you’re doing and always err on caution. When in doubt, hire a professional to do the job.

When it comes to electrical repairs, there are a few things that you should never try to do on your own. These include:

-Working with live wires

-Attempting to repair or replace a circuit breaker

-Trying to fix an issue with your home’s main electrical panel

If you’re uncomfortable working with electricity, it’s best to leave the repairs to a professional. Trying to do them yourself could put you and your family at risk.

3. You Might Make the Problem Worse


If you don’t know what you’re doing, there’s a good chance you’ll make the problem worse. This could result in further damage to your home and increased safety risks. And, of course, it will also cost you more money in the long run.

If you have the time and knowledge, you may be able to tackle some of the repairs yourself. But for most homeowners, it’s best to leave it to the professionals.

Regarding home repairs, some jobs are too risky or technical for the average person. These are the types of repairs that you should always leave to a professional:

Structural Repairs: Any repair that affects the load-bearing structure of your home should be done by a professional. This includes foundation repairs, framing repairs, and roofing repairs.

Electrical Repairs: Electrical work is hazardous and should only be done by a licensed electrician. This includes anything from changing a lightbulb to rewiring your home.

When it comes down to it, electrical repairs are simply not worth the risk. Even if you know what you’re doing, it’s always best to play it safe and leave repairs to the professionals. Not only will this protect you from injury, but it will also protect your home from damage.
